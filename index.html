How I engineer

A note on working in the AI era — since it's changing what "senior" actually means.

01

Judgment over output

AI writes more of the boilerplate every year. What doesn't change is who decides the tradeoffs — that's still my job, and it's the part I lean into.

02

Architecture first

Every feature starts with "what breaks at 10x scale." At 2M+ users, that question isn't theoretical.

03

Review is mentorship

Code review is how a team gets better, not just how code gets cleaner. I treat it as teaching time.

04

Ship, then harden

Reliability is a habit built release over release — not a milestone you hit once and move on from.
